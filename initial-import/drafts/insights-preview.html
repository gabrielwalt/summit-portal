Top insight

Experience and SEO health tell 2 different stories in the same audit.

Shoppers feel a 22 average lab experience while SEO health reads 67. That gap is revenue and brand risk when traffic slips.

Experience avg: 22 | SEO health: 67

Tie funding to closing the gap, not to whichever metric looks better in isolation.

Demand

Organic visits are down about 12 percent year over year.

Seasonality still moves the line, but the year-over-year slope is not your friend when branded share is this high.

Apr: 58M | May: 55M | Jun: 60M | Jul: 62M | Aug: 65M | Sep: 61M | Oct: 58M | Nov: 55M | Dec: 54M | Jan: 53M | Feb: 52M | Mar: 56M

Peak near 65.5 M in this modeled year, recent months closer to 52 to 56 M.

Concentration

More than 9 in 10 organic visits still come from branded queries.

That is efficient until volume weakens. You need a deliberate non-branded plan before you feel the hit in revenue.

Branded share of modeled organic queries: 92%

Many healthy portfolios aim for a larger non-branded slice over time without giving up branded strength.

AI visibility

Roughly 3.84 M sampled citations exist, but they skew hard to one surface.

Google AI Mode dominates the split. Visibility is real; diversification of answers and prompts is the unlock.

Google AI Overviews: 8% | Perplexity: 5% | ChatGPT: 3% | Google AI Mode: 84%

Invest in structured answers and landing paths assistants can cite with confidence.

Capital

The same 4 templates carry most of the story and most of the pain.

Women's, homepage, Jordan, and Nike By You cluster in the twenties while Women's collapses in lab. Fund them as one program.

Women's: 18 | Homepage: 24 | Jordan: 22 | Nike By You: 26

Scores are lab-based; use RUM on these URLs before you scale patterns everywhere.
